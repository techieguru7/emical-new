How EMI Calculator App Works

An EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is a fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender at a specified date each calendar month. It's used to repay a loan, usually including both the principal amount and interest. An EMI Calculator App helps users calculate the EMI amount based on the loan amount, interest rate, and loan tenure.

Input Data:

When users open the EMI Calculator App, they are presented with an interface that allows them to input the following data:

Loan Amount:

The total amount they want to borrow. Interest Rate: The annual interest rate at which the loan is borrowed. Loan Tenure: The duration of the loan in months or years.

Interest Rate:

The annual interest rate at which the loan is borrowed.

Loan Tenure:

The duration of the loan in months or years.

Calculation of EMI:

Once the user enters the required data, the app calculates the EMI using the following formula:

EMI = [P * r * (1 + r)^n] / [(1 + r)^n - 1]

Where:

P is the principal loan amount

r is the monthly interest rate (annual rate divided by 12 and then by 100)

n is the number of EMIs (loan tenure in months)

Display of Results:

The calculated EMI is then displayed to the user, providing a clear understanding of the fixed monthly payment they'll need to make towards repaying the loan.

Extra EMI

An Extra EMI is an additional payment made by the borrower on top of the regular EMI. This extra payment goes directly towards reducing the principal amount of the loan.

Annual Extra EMI:

"Extra EMI annually" means that you make these additional payments once a year, usually on the same date or around the anniversary of taking out the loan.

Benefits of Extra EMI Annually:

Faster Loan Repayment:

The extra payments reduce the outstanding principal, which in turn decreases the interest accrued on the loan. This leads to a shorter loan tenure.

Reduced Interest Paid:

By paying off the loan faster, you reduce the total interest paid over the loan's lifetime. Financial Freedom: Paying off your loan early gives you more financial freedom and security.

Total Interest Payable

"Total Interest Payable" is a key financial term associated with loans and loan repayment, often used in the context of an EMI calculator. It refers to the cumulative amount of interest that a borrower will pay over the entire duration of a loan, in addition to repaying the principal amount.
